Cuisines of India

India's cuisine is incredibly diverse, with distinct regional styles. Here's a brief overview of the main categories:

North Indian: Known for rich, creamy gravies and use of dairy products like ghee and paneer. Staple foods include wheat-based breads like naan and roti. Key dishes are butter chicken and dal makhani.

South Indian: Characterized by a greater use of rice, coconut, and tamarind. The food is often spicier and features a lot of curry leaves. Staple dishes are dosa, idli, and sambar.

East Indian: Relies heavily on rice and fish, with mustard oil as a common cooking medium. This region, particularly Bengal, is famous for its sweets and fish curries.

West Indian: Features a mix of flavors. Gujarati cuisine is primarily vegetarian with a sweet and sour taste, while Maharashtrian food includes popular street snacks like vada pav and pav bhaji. Goan cuisine is known for its seafood dishes and use of vinegar, with Goan fish curry and chicken xacuti as famous examples.
