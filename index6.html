Famous Monuments

Monuments are historical and architectural symbols of human history and culture. Here are some of the most famous:

The Great Wall of China: A massive series of ancient fortifications in China, built to protect against invasions. It is a symbol of Chinese history and engineering.

The Pyramids of Giza (Egypt): Ancient tombs for pharaohs, including the Great Pyramid and the Sphinx. They are a testament to ancient Egypt's civilization and power.

The Colosseum (Italy): A huge Roman amphitheater where gladiatorial contests and public spectacles were held. It is an icon of Imperial Rome's architectural genius.

The Taj Mahal (India): A white marble mausoleum built in the 17th century by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife. It is a stunning example of Mughal architecture and a symbol of eternal love.

The Eiffel Tower (France): A wrought-iron lattice tower in Paris, built for the 1889 World's Fair. It has become a global cultural icon of France.

Machu Picchu (Peru): A 15th-century Inca citadel in the Andes Mountains, known for its impressive dry-stone architecture and stunning mountain views.

Stonehenge (United Kingdom): A prehistoric ring of standing stones in England, whose purpose remains a subject of archaeological debate.
